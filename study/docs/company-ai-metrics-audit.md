# What Companies Actually Measure: AI-Assisted Development Metrics Audit

**Date:** 2026-03-28
**Purpose:** Document the gap between what companies track (generation metrics) and what they don't (outcome metrics).

## The Pattern

Every company measures generation. Almost nobody measures whether it worked.

| What everyone tracks | What almost nobody tracks |
|---------------------|--------------------------|
| PRs merged / merge rate | Rework rate (any definition) |
| % of code AI-generated | Change failure rate by AI origin |
| Time saved (self-reported) | Escaped defects by origin |
| Adoption rate (DAU/WAU) | Cost per accepted change |
| Task completion speed | Abandoned work |
| | Review burden increase |

## Company-by-Company

### Spotify (Honk)
- **Published:** 1,500+ merged PRs, 650+/month, 60-90% time savings on migrations, judge veto rate ~25%
- **Missing:** No CFR, no rework rate, no escaped defect tracking, no cost-per-change, no quality comparison AI vs human post-merge
- **Source:** Engineering blog (Nov-Dec 2025), QCon London 2026

### Stripe
- **Published:** CI wait time reduced 70% (3.5h/week → less), 20% more features shipped
- **Missing:** Almost nothing about AI coding impact specifically. No AI adoption rates, no quality data, no rework
- **Source:** Engineering blog, stripe.dev

### Uber (uReview)
- **Published:** 90% of 65K weekly diffs covered, 75% usefulness rating, 65% of AI comments addressed, ~1,500 hours/week saved
- **Missing:** No data on whether uReview catches defects that would have escaped. No CFR comparison. "Usefulness" is self-reported, not validated against outcomes
- **Source:** Uber Engineering Blog (Sep 2025)

### Dropbox
- **Published:** DAU/WAU, satisfaction scores, time saved, AI spend, PR throughput by AI usage cohort
- **Missing:** No CFR, no rework, no escaped defects, no cost-per-change. Acknowledges they're trying to connect to "tangible business outcomes" but haven't yet
- **Source:** DX case study, executive roundtable

### Anthropic (Claude Code)
- **Published:** 200K transcripts analyzed, task complexity 3.2→3.8, feature implementation 14.3%→36.9%, PR output ~200% YoY, Code Review 16%→54% findings, <1% incorrect
- **Missing:** No CFR, no rework, no escaped defects, no cost-per-change. 200% PR increase presented without quality controls. Most rigorous methodology of any company but still input-focused
- **Source:** Research paper (Aug 2025)

### Google (Gemini)
- **Published:** "25% of all new code is AI-generated" (Pichai, earnings call)
- **Missing:** Zero methodology. No quality comparison. No CFR. No rework. The 25% figure is investor relations, not engineering measurement
- **Source:** Q3 2024 earnings call

### Microsoft (Copilot)
- **Published:** 55% faster task completion (4,800 devs), 46% code generated by Copilot, 81% productivity improvement, 3.6h saved/week, 4.7M paid subscribers
- **Missing:** No CFR comparison. No rework. No escaped defects. "81% productivity improvement" has no disclosed methodology. GitClear independently found 4x code clone growth — Microsoft hasn't addressed it
- **Source:** Microsoft InsideTrack, research studies

### Amazon (Q Developer)
- **Published:** "4,500 developer-years saved," 24% reduction in time to PR, 142% increase in monthly PRs
- **Missing:** "4,500 developer-years" has no methodology. No CFR. No rework. No quality comparison. Dashboard metrics are input-focused
- **Source:** AWS DevOps Blog

### Shopify
- **Published:** Tobi's autoresearch experiment (120 iterations, 53% faster Liquid), cultural mandate ("reflexive AI usage is baseline"), hiring requires justifying why AI can't do the job
- **Missing:** Zero systematic engineering metrics. No PR throughput. No rework. No CFR. Entirely CEO showmanship, not measurement
- **Source:** Simon Willison coverage, First Round, Pragmatic Engineer

### Booking.com
- **Published:** 16% higher PR merge rate for daily AI users, 150K dev hours saved, 65% adoption, DX Core 4 framework (Speed, Effectiveness, Quality, Impact)
- **Missing:** Names CFR as a dimension but hasn't published the numbers. Has the most complete framework but published the least about quality outcomes
- **Source:** DX case study, QCon London 2026

### Coinbase
- **Published:** 40% AI-generated code, every engineer uses Cursor, monthly metrics to leaders (lead time, deployment frequency, bugs, incidents, AI usage)
- **Missing:** No published bug/incident trend data since AI adoption. Track internally but don't disclose direction
- **Source:** Coinbase DevX blog

### OpenAI (Codex)
- **Published:** 70% more PRs merged weekly, 3.5 PRs/engineer/day, 72.1% SWE-bench, "quality maintained"
- **Missing:** "Quality maintained" has no supporting data. No CFR. No rework. 3.5 PRs/engineer/day is an extraordinary claim with no methodology
- **Source:** openai.com

## Industry Surveys

### Cortex 2026 Benchmark
- PRs per author up 20% YoY
- **Incidents per PR up 23.5%**
- **Change failure rates up ~30%**
- Only 45% have formal AI governance
- Title: "AI Is Making Engineering Faster, But Not Better"

### Faros AI (10,000+ developers, 1,255 teams)
- 98% more PRs, 154% larger, 91% longer review times
- Correlation between AI adoption and performance "evaporates at the company level"
- 66% say biggest frustration is AI solutions "almost right, but not quite"
- 45% find debugging AI code more time-consuming than writing it

### DX (400+ companies)
- **Only 20% of teams use engineering metrics to measure AI impact**
- Companies measure adoption but not outcomes

### Jellyfish (2M+ PRs)
- 51% of PRs used AI by May 2025
- AI use up 260% YoY
- **60% of leaders cite "lack of clear metrics" as their biggest AI challenge**

### METR (16 developers, 246 tasks)
- Experienced devs **19% slower** with AI
- Believed they were 20% faster
- Perception-reality gap is the finding
